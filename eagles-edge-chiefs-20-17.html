Eagles Edge Chiefs 20-17 in Super Bowl Rematch

Philadelphia survives Kansas City’s comeback attempt with defense and late heroics.

Published September 15, 2025

The Philadelphia Eagles leaned on resilience and a smothering defense to outlast the Kansas City Chiefs, 20-17, in a highly anticipated rematch of Super Bowl LVII.

  • Safety Andrew Mukuba delivered the momentum swing with a key interception, setting up a short-field score.
  • Jalen Hurts kept drives alive with his legs and the “Tush Push,” extending a crucial 4th-and-goal.
  • Kansas City’s mistakes proved costly — Travis Kelce dropped a would-be touchdown, and miscommunications slowed Mahomes.

    • The Eagles improve to 2-0, reminding the NFL that their championship window is wide open. For Kansas City, a 0-2 start raises questions about their supporting cast.
