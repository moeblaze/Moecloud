From Zero to First Recording (5 Minutes)

By Moe Bucks • 6 min read

  1. Open Haz‑AL and load a video.
  2. Load samples into banks and test playback.
  3. Use an FX (Glitch, Spark Trail, VHS, etc.).
  4. Click Image Splash and pick an overlay.
  5. Hit Record → 3‑2‑1 countdown → export MP4.

Pro Tips

  • Keep overlays 1080×1920 (9:16) for shorts; 1920×1080 for landscape.
  • Chain FX sparingly to avoid clipping audio.
  • Use consistent naming: intro_splash_01.png, fx_vhs_01.

What is Haz‑AL?

Haz‑AL is a creator tool for fast video reactions with live FX, image splash overlays, 16-bank sample triggers, and a built‑in 3‑2‑1 recorder that exports MP4.

Core Features

  • Video Loader — MP4/MOV playback with precise scrub.
  • Sample Banks (16) — load WAV/MP3/AIF; one‑tap triggers.
  • FX Suite — Glitch, Spark Trail, Particles, Heatwave, Comic Bubbles, Robot Mask, Animal Ears, Mirror Bounce, VHS, Neon, Mood Warm, Mood Noir, Trail Echo, Screen Crack, Pop Text, Light Burst.
  • Image Splash — overlay PNG/JPG/GIF full‑frame splash on beat.
  • Recorder — 3‑2‑1 countdown; capture video+audio; export MP4.
  • Layouts — 16:9 and 9:16 friendly composition.

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Space — Play/Pause video
  • ← / → — Scrub backward/forward
  • 1–4 rows × QWER/ASDF/ZXCV/1234 (example) — trigger sample banks (map may vary by build)
  • R — Start/Stop recording (opens 3‑2‑1)
  • I — Image Splash picker
  • F — Cycle FX; Shift+F to clear

Best‑Practice Workflow

  1. Load your base video, confirm audio levels.
  2. Load 4–8 go‑to samples (intros, stingers, tags) into the first row.
  3. Pre‑select 2–3 FX you’ll actually use to keep it clean.
  4. Prep 3–5 image splashes sized to your aspect ratio.
  5. Hit Record → perform live → export MP4 and publish.

Troubleshooting

  • No audio in export — ensure system/ tab audio permissions are allowed; verify input device; try reload.
  • FX not visible — check that the video is playing and FX layer is toggled on.
  • Image splash not covering frame — use 1920×1080 (16:9) or 1080×1920 (9:16) assets.
  • Large MP4 size — trim length, limit stacked FX, and compress post‑export if needed.

FAQ

Q: What files can I load for samples?
WAV, MP3, or AIFF typically work best.

Q: Can I add my own overlays?
Yes — upload PNG/JPG with transparency preferred for clean edges.

Q: Does recording capture my mic?
Yes if enabled; select your mic in the app or system settings.
