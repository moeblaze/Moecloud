Weekend Track & Field Preview — Jefferson‑Wooden vs. Everyone

Melissa Jefferson‑Wooden enters the weekend as the heavy favorite in the women’s 100m. Can Sha’Carri Richardson reset and fight her way back onto the podium?

Predictions Inside
Jump to Predictions ↓

Context & Recent Form

  • Melissa Jefferson‑Wooden has been dominant all season. Her 100m personal best is 10.65 s, making her joint‑5th fastest woman ever in that event.
  • She has been undefeated in the 100m this season, winning every major race she’s entered, including Grand Slam Track meets and Diamond League events.
  • Besides her 100m, she’s also performed strongly in the 200m—for example, her 21.84 s win at USATF outdoors.

Your call from last year — “nobody will beat her this year” — is aging well.

Sha’Carri Richardson: Can She Recalculate?

Sha’Carri Richardson remains one of the most gifted sprinters in the world, but her recent form and off‑track issues have made the path tougher.

Recent issues:

  • She was arrested in late July on a domestic violence charge involving Christian Coleman.
  • She competed in the opening 100m round at the US Championships but withdrew from the semifinals. She also did not make the final in the 200m.

Obstacles:

  • The mental and emotional impact of the arrest and its aftermath can disrupt training and execution.
  • Her recent race times have not matched her best, with fewer sub‑11s this season.
  • With Jefferson‑Wooden in peak form, the bar for a podium is high.

Upcoming Races / Key Matchups

  • Diamond League Brussels features a marquee 100m with Jefferson‑Wooden, Richardson, and Shelly‑Ann Fraser‑Pryce — a final tune‑up before the big show.
  • The World Championships (Tokyo) loom, with Richardson holding an auto‑entry in the 100m as defending world champion and Jefferson‑Wooden among the gold‑medal favorites.
